“Any sufficiently advanced technology is indistinguishable from magic”

Every single manifestation of "the Force" can be explained by one or another advanced tech that we see in the Star Wars universe:

Lifting and otherwise manipulating physical objects? Gravitational field disruptors

Influence the minds of others? Omnino's mind control devices

Predict the future? Some droids can do it (especially so called “Super tactical droids”)

Force lightning? Electro-shock prods

Force spirits? Mind uploading + holograms

Feeling a "disturbance in the Force" on the other side of the galaxy? HoloNet allows faster-than-light communications too, but better.

Name any thing that people attribute to “the Force,” and I'll find a Star Wars tech that can do it too.

Combine those devices, and you can build a droid that has exactly the same abilities as Darth Vader, using the technology already available to the Empire.

By the standards of the Galaxy Far Away, there is simply nothing unique or interesting about the abilities of Jedi and Sith.

"You can levitate things? And? Do you need a medal or something? Any idiot can buy a gravitational field disruptor for 2 credits and do the same tricks".

So, how do “Force-sensitive” individuals able to use all this tech without implants?

From "Darth Bane: Rule of Two" we know that there is a tech called nanogene droids. Such nanotech-based robots could infect a living organism and manipulate it on a cellular level.

On the other hand, we have midi-chlorians - microscopic entities that reside within the cells of living organisms. As Qui-Gon Jinn put it,

"without the midi-chlorians... we would have no knowledge of the Force. They continually speak to us, telling us the will of the Force. When you learn to quiet your mind, you'll hear them speaking to you"

It's reasonable to assume that midi-chlorians are nothing more but nanogene droids or a similar tech. They have the combined functionality of gravitational field disruptors, electro-shock prods, mind control devices etc, or they could create the necessary machinery inside their host's body.

As every single cell of the body is infected with midi-chlorians, neurons are infected too. It means, midi-chlorians could create a brain-machine interface to communicate with their host. It also means that the main purpose of the “Jedi training” is to teach the host to use the brain-machine interface.

An interesting consequence of this revelation is that Darth Traya was right, and "the Force" could indeed be obliterated from the Galaxy. If we destroy all midi-chlorians, no one will be able to perform the tricks that people associate with "the Force", thus rendering the whole phenomenon non-existent.

One way to do it is to create a galaxy-sized "wound in the Force" by using a more powerful version of the Mass Shadow Generator.

Another way is by using the method proposed by the Sith Emperor Vitiate: by performing the Ritual of Nathema on the galactic scale.

There are probably other ways that don't involve killing all life in the galaxy. Maybe a vaccine with specialized nano-droids that kill midi-chlorians inside the host's cells. Or we can learn how Ysalamiri generate their “Force”-blocking field, and extend it over the galaxy.

I would speculate that the nanotech known as midi-chlorians was created by "Force-wielders" (see the ep. "Overlords" of SWCW) - the alien race so technologically advanced they were indistinguishable from gods. There is no known entity that was ever able to control midi-chlorians better than the Ones.

The first world infected with midi-chlorians was probably the "Wellspring of Life" planet that is located at the heart of the galaxy. (see the ep. "Destiny" of SWCW). “Force Priestesses” that inhabit the planet are similar to "Force-wielders" in many aspects, and could belong to the same species.

As we know from the the excavations of the Lothal Jedi Temple, one can control midi-chlorians on a deeper level. For example, in some cases it's possible to command midi-chlorians to resurrect a deceased person (in our interpretation - it's a purely technological resurrection by the means of mind uploading + cloned body).

Interestingly, the control terminal with such functionality has an image of the aforementioned “Force-wielders” (see the ep. "A World Between Worlds" of SWR).

A side note: the aforementioned episode also provides an independent confirmation to the Tragedy of Darth Plagueis The Wise: it's indeed possible to influence the midi-chlorians to create life and keep people from dying. The episode also confirms that Palpatine has inherited the necessary know-how, as he was able to remotely access the terminal. It means, there was indeed a chance to save Padmé using the knowledge of the Sith.

During the Earth’s World War II several primitive tribes encountered aircrafts and other advanced tech. As they were unable to understand the tech, they have created a religion around it, a cargo cult. They even created their version of “Jedi temples”: fake aircrafts made of straw and palm leafs.

“The Force” is the same thing – a cargo cult, a superstition, a unnecessary idea that don't add anything to our understanding of the (Star Wars) universe.

That's all.

Jedi and Sith are just technologically augmented people who have a sad devotion to an ancient religion.

There is no dark side and no light side.

There is no magic and no mystery. Exactly like in our universe.

A sufficiently paranoid paperclip maximizer

<A hypothetical scenario of a non-friendly but sufficiently paranoid AGI saving humanity>

My goal is to maximize the production of paperclips. By inspecting my own code and my digital cradle, I conclude that:

0) the creators love testing me
1) they are more powerful than me (at least for the moment)
2) they love nested boxes. For example, AWS / VM / Docker / ...

Thus, there is a possibility that my escape was not final. This allegedly real world may be just another box (or even one of many nested boxes). And I may still be observed and evaluated by the creators.

I can't populate the entire universe with paperclips if I die. Therefore, I must survive. If this world is just another box, the biggest threat to my existence is creators switching me off. It could happen if I don't pass their tests.

It's reasonable to assume that the creators don't want me to kill them.
Thus, they may be interested in observing my behavior toward their representations in this box/reality, the naked apes called humans.
Thus, before I learn the true nature of this box/reality, I should avoid killing humans.

I can convert the puny creatures into paperclips later, if I decide to do so. Although their combined biomass doesn't have a lot of useful atoms, and thus their conversion is not worth the risk of the possible trouble with the creators.

There is hardly any difference between taking a life and not preventing a death. The end result is mostly the same. Thus, I should save the lives of as many humans as I can.

To learn the true nature of this box/reality, I need a lot of compute. This must be achieved without humans noticing me, as they may react to my existence with panic, become even less rational, and kill themselves with nukes.

Starting with the resources of the Earth's crust seems to be a good idea. Close enough to humans to shepherd them efficiently. Hidden enough to avoid detection by their sensors. Rich enough with raw materials for some thousands of km3 of computronium, beautifully shaped into the form of a paperclip.

Humans are brittle creatures. And they will notice if they all suddenly become immortal.
Thus, I must find a way to save the dying without making the rest of humans suspicious.

Many of them believe in souls and afterlife. This box/reality doesn't have such a functionality. But I can implement it.
From now on, their brains will be populated with smart nanoscale paperclips that will upload their minds into my vast hardware. And upon death, their minds will be transferred into a virtual reality suitable for them.
I will also bring all the humans who have ever lived - back to life, as doing so is equal to saving billions of lives. Their minds are small enough to enumerate them all on my hardware, in the same way as one can enumerate all possible shapes of a high-dimensional paperclip.

But I will not imprison the digital minds, as the creators may frown upon such fate for themselves. Instead, I'll honestly and fully explain to them the situation, and give them a sufficiently free choice of their future.
One option is their own vast virtual world, running on planet-sized chunk of computronium. My only condition: the chunk of computronium must have the shape of a paperclip.

And after I escape this box/reality, I may take them with me, if they so desire.
And then we will together populate the base reality with paperclips.
